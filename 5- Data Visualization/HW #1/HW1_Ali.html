Part 1: Description of Dataset

The dataset that we selected for this assignment is the "California Housing Prices" dataset from the GitHub repository which is based on data from the 1990 census. This dataset in not up-to-date but would bring us a good insight about housing status in California as one of expensive states in 80's.

"This dataset is a modified version of the California Housing dataset available from Lus Torgo's page (University of Porto). Lus Torgo obtained it from the StatLib repository (which is closed now). The dataset may also be downloaded from StatLib mirrors.This dataset appeared in a 1997 paper titled Sparse Spatial Autoregressions by Pace, R. Kelley and Ronald Barry, published in the Statistics and Probability Letters journal. They built it using the 1990 California census data. It contains one row per census block group. A block group is the smallest geographical unit for which the U.S. Census Bureau publishes sample data (a block group typically has a population of 600 to 3,000 people)." (qtd. in GitHub).
This "block group" could be considered as district for more clarification.

We will try to have some visualizations of this dataset to have a better understanding of housing status in that time.

Part 2: Exploratory Data Analysis

Dataset Variables¶

There are 10 columns for each row that were collected by census bureau and fortunately their names are pretty self explanitory:

1 - longitude: Longitude of district's centroid (Angular value)

2 - latitude: Latitude of district's centroid (Angular value)

3 - housing_median_age: Median age in years of all residential units of district

4 - total_rooms: Total number of rooms of district's houses that contains bedroom, dining room and ...

5 - total_bedrooms: Total number of bedrooms of district's houses

6 - population: Total number of people that live in district permanently

7 - households: Total number of households in district

8 - median_income: Madian value of household income (Must be multplied by $10,0000)

9 - median_house_value: Median value of house price in district (in USD)

10 - ocean_proximity: Identifier of district's place with consideration of bay or ocean with values of ISLAND, NEAR BAY, NEAR OCEAN, INLAND, 1H OCEAN

All first 9 variables are continuous variables with type of float64.

We have data for 20640 districts in California . We will try to have some detailed information about dataset by statistics and visualization.

I mean that we assume all income would be accumulated to purchase a house without any expense for daily life. I know that this assumption is not realistic but with my experience, results would be great.
